# REAL PROPERTY CONTRAST TABLES

*Clean comparisons for MBE exam traps. Columns: Element | Outcome Trigger | Remedy | Exam Trap | 1-liner Hypo*

---

## DEFEASIBLE FEES COMPARISON

| Element | Fee Simple Determinable (FSD) | Fee Simple Subject to Condition Subsequent (FSCS) | Executory Limitation |
|---------|------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|---------------------|
| **Termination** | Automatic on condition | Manual reentry required | Automatic transfer to third party |
| **Outcome Trigger** | "So long as" language | "But if" language | "Provided that" + third party |
| **Remedy** | Possibility of reverter | Right of reentry | Executory interest |
| **Exam Trap** | Confusing automatic vs. manual | Thinking FSCS is automatic | Missing third-party element |
| **1-liner Hypo** | "To A so long as used for school" → FSD; condition ends it automatically | "To A, but if used for business" → FSCS; grantor must act to retake | "To A, provided that if sold, to B" → Executory; automatic to B |

## EASEMENT TYPES COMPARISON

| Element | Appurtenant Easement | Easement in Gross | License |
|---------|----------------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Attachment** | Benefits dominant estate | Benefits individual | Personal permission |
| **Outcome Trigger** | "Appurtenant to" language | Personal to holder | Oral/written permission |
| **Remedy** | Runs with land transfer | Doesn't run, ends at death | Revocable anytime |
| **Exam Trap** | Forgetting dominant/servient requirement | Thinking in gross runs with land | Treating license as property interest |
| **1-liner Hypo** | "Right to cross Grantor's land" → Appurtenant; transfers with dominant lot | "Utility company pole right" → In gross; personal to company | "You may cross my land today" → License; revocable |

## COVENANTS VS SERVITUDES COMPARISON

| Element | Real Covenant | Equitable Servitude |
|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Creation** | Intent + notice + touch & concern + privity | Intent + notice + touch & concern |
| **Outcome Trigger** | Vertical privity (landowner to landowner) | No privity requirement |
| **Remedy** | Money damages | Injunctive relief |
| **Exam Trap** | Requiring horizontal privity for servitudes | Thinking damages available for servitudes |
| **1-liner Hypo** | Promise not to build; successor sues for breach → Real covenant; damages | Same promise; court enforces against successor → Equitable servitude; injunction |

### COMMON SCHEME (IMPLIED EQUITABLE SERVITUDE) VS EXPRESS COVENANT

| Element | Common Scheme Servitude | Express Covenant |
|---------|------------------------|------------------|
| **Source** | Subdivision plan + IRIS factors (Intent, Restrictive promise, Notice, Same scheme) | Written promise recorded in deed |
| **Notice Needed** | Actual, record, or inquiry (patterns, signage, plat map) | Actual/record via deed itself |
| **Who Is Bound** | All lots in scheme (even early sales) if IRIS satisfied | Only parties and successors with notice/privity |
| **Remedy** | Injunction to stop non-conforming use | Damages (real covenant) or injunction (servitude) |
| **Exam Trap** | Assuming Lot 1 is free because deed silent | Forgetting to check for common restrictions in subdivision |
| **1-liner Hypo** | Lot sold first, deed silent, but street of "Residential Only" homes → Clinic barred | Deed says "residential only" → Express covenant; enforceable |

## CONCURRENT OWNERSHIP COMPARISON

| Element | Joint Tenancy | Tenancy in Common | Tenancy by Entirety |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Unities Required** | All 4 (time, title, interest, possession) | None required | All 4 + valid marriage |
| **Outcome Trigger** | "Joint tenants" language | "Tenants in common" or unequal shares | Married couple owners |
| **Remedy** | Right of survivorship | No survivorship; partition available | Survivorship + creditor protection |
| **Exam Trap** | Missing unity destroys joint tenancy | Presumption favors tenancy in common | Death doesn't sever (triggers survivorship) |
| **1-liner Hypo** | "To A and B as joint tenants" → Survivorship applies | "To A (1/2) and B (1/2)" → No survivorship | Married owners; one dies → Other gets all automatically |

## ASSIGNMENT VS SUBLEASE COMPARISON

| Element | Assignment | Sublease |
|---------|------------|----------|
| **Transfer** | Entire remaining lease term | Portion of lease term |
| **Outcome Trigger** | Transferee assumes all obligations | Original tenant remains liable |
| **Remedy** | Privity of estate with landlord | Privity of contract only |
| **Exam Trap** | Thinking sublease ends original liability | Landlord must approve assignment unless waived |
| **1-liner Hypo** | Tenant transfers entire 2-year lease → Assignment; new tenant liable to landlord | Tenant rents space to subtenant for 6 months → Sublease; original tenant still liable |

## RECORDING ACTS COMPARISON

| Element | Race Statute | Notice Statute | Race-Notice Statute |
|---------|--------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Winner** | First to record | First bona fide purchaser without notice | First to record without notice |
| **Outcome Trigger** | No notice inquiry | Notice defeats protection | Must record + no notice |
| **Remedy** | Recording priority | Notice priority | Recording + notice protection |
| **Exam Trap** | Thinking race protects with notice | Notice alone defeats BFP | Forgetting notice requirement |
| **1-liner Hypo** | First records wins regardless → Race statute | BFP without notice wins over recorded deed → Notice statute | First to record without notice wins → Race-notice (majority) |

## ADDITIONAL CONTRAST TABLES

### MORTGAGE VS DEED OF TRUST

| Element | Mortgage | Deed of Trust |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| **Title Holder** | Mortgagor retains title | Trustee holds title |
| **Outcome Trigger** | "Mortgage" language | "Deed of trust" + trustee |
| **Remedy** | Judicial foreclosure | Non-judicial foreclosure (faster) |
| **Exam Trap** | Thinking mortgage transfers title | Trustee powers in default |
| **1-liner Hypo** | Lender gets mortgage on home → Mortgagor keeps title; judicial process | Same with trustee → Trustee holds title; faster foreclosure |

### PHYSICAL VS REGULATORY TAKING

| Element | Physical Taking | Regulatory Taking |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Government Action** | Physical invasion | Regulation diminishes value |
| **Outcome Trigger** | Actual entry/taking | Substantial diminution |
| **Remedy** | Just compensation always | Compensation if Penn Central factors met |
| **Exam Trap** | Temporary takings require compensation | Reasonable regulations don't require compensation |
| **1-liner Hypo** | Government floods land for project → Physical taking; compensation | Zoning reduces value 50% → Regulatory taking if factors met |

### AFFIRMATIVE VS NEGATIVE EASEMENTS

| Element | Affirmative Easement | Negative Easement |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Right Type** | Right to use/do something | Right to prevent something |
| **Outcome Trigger** | "Right to cross/drive/build" | "Right to light/air/support" |
| **Remedy** | Access/use enforcement | Injunction against interference |
| **Exam Trap** | Negative easements require writing | Scope limited to necessity |
| **1-liner Hypo** | "Right to drive across land" → Affirmative; permits use | "Right to prevent building blocking light" → Negative; prevents action |

### PRESENT VS FUTURE INTERESTS

| Element | Present Interest | Future Interest |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Possession** | Immediate right to possess | Possession delayed |
| **Outcome Trigger** | "To A" (immediate) | "Then to B" (conditional/future) |
| **Remedy** | Current enjoyment | Waits for condition |
| **Exam Trap** | Reversion is future interest | Contingent remainders must vest |
| **1-liner Hypo** | "To A for life" → A has present life estate | "Remainder to A's children" → Future interest in children |
