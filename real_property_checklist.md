# REAL PROPERTY POCKET CHECKLISTS

*Quick-reference workflows. Check each element for complete analysis.*

---

## DEED VALIDITY CHECKLIST

**Essential Elements:**
□ Grantor has legal capacity
□ Grantor owns the property
□ Delivery + acceptance (intent to convey)
□ Adequate description of property
□ Signed by grantor
□ Witnesses/notarization (if required)

**Common Defects:**
□ Undelivered deed (wild deed)
□ Mental incapacity
□ Forgery/fraud/duress
□ After-acquired title issues
□ Missing legal description
□ Lack of intent to convey

**Deed Types:**
□ Warranty: 6 covenants of title
□ Special warranty: limited covenants
□ Quitclaim: no warranties
□ Bargain & sale: some covenants

---

## RECORDING WORKFLOW

**BFP Status Check:**
□ Good faith belief in ownership
□ Valuable consideration paid
□ No actual notice of prior claims
□ No constructive notice (recorded docs)
□ No inquiry notice (facts requiring investigation)

**Recording Act Application:**
□ Race statute: first to record wins
□ Notice statute: no notice protects
□ Race-notice (majority): record + no notice
□ Chain of title: examine entire chain

**Defenses to Protection:**
□ Wild/undelivered deeds
□ Forged instruments
□ Lack of valuable consideration
□ Actual knowledge of prior rights
□ Inquiry notice triggers

## FIXTURES TEST (FIX)

**Attachment (F):**
□ Physically fastened to land?
□ Method of attachment (bolts, cement, etc.)?
□ Can be removed without damage?

**Intent (I):**
□ Intended for permanent improvement?
□ Not just temporary or personal use?
□ Business equipment = trade fixture?

**Extent (X):**
□ Removal would damage property?
□ Integral to realty function?
□ Cost vs. benefit of removal?

**Special Cases:**
□ Trade fixtures: tenant removable
□ Mortgage liens: attach to fixtures
□ Agricultural: special removal rights
□ Building materials: fixtures when incorporated

## COVENANT/SERVITUDE RUN ELEMENTS

**Intent:**
□ Parties intended burden/benefit to run?
□ Express language of succession?
□ Implied from common plan?

**Notice:**
□ Actual knowledge?
□ Constructive notice (recorded)?
□ Inquiry notice from circumstances?

**Touch & Concern:**
□ Affects land use/value?
□ Personal to individuals?
□ Not just neighborhood amenity?

**Privity:**
□ Real covenant: vertical privity required
□ Equitable servitude: no privity needed
□ Horizontal privity: original parties

**Running Rules:**
□ Burden runs: intent + notice + touch & concern + privity
□ Benefit runs: same elements
□ Equity: court enforcement vs. damages

## JOINT-TENANCY SEVERANCE CHECK

**4 Unities Present:**
□ Time: same moment acquired
□ Title: same instrument
□ Interest: equal shares
□ Possession: shared use rights

**Severance Methods:**
□ Conveyance by joint tenant
□ Mortgage by joint tenant
□ Partition action filed
□ Death (triggers survivorship)

**Post-Severance:**
□ Becomes tenancy in common
□ No survivorship rights
□ Partition available
□ Unequal shares possible

**Special Cases:**
□ Tenancy by entirety: marital protection
□ Creditor's lien: may sever
□ Divorce: often severs entirety
□ Foreign joint tenancy: check local law

## IMPLIED WARRANTY OF HABITABILITY (IWH)

**Landlord Duties:**
□ Comply with building codes
□ Maintain structural components
□ Provide heat/plumbing/electricity
□ Safe and sanitary conditions
□ No violations at lease start

**Tenant Remedies:**
□ Withhold rent (repair & deduct)
□ Terminate lease
□ Sue for damages
□ Repair and deduct costs

**Exceptions:**
□ Commercial leases
□ Tenant caused damage
□ Landlord unaware of issues
□ Short-term rentals (<1 year)

---

## QUIET ENJOYMENT (QE) WARRANTY

**Landlord Duties:**
□ No interference with possession
□ Protect against third-party claims
□ Maintain exclusive use rights
□ No lockouts or utility shutoffs

**Tenant Remedies:**
□ Withhold rent
□ Terminate lease
□ Sue for damages
□ Injunctive relief

**Key Differences from IWH:**
□ QE: third-party interference
□ IWH: landlord's maintenance duty
□ QE: breach by acts/omissions
□ IWH: habitability standards

## MORTGAGE PRIORITY FLOW

**Purchase Money Priority:**
□ Borrowed to purchase property?
□ Recorded immediately?
□ Used for down payment/acquisition?
□ Construction loan funds?

**Later Liens Priority:**
□ Recorded first in time?
□ First in time, first in right
□ Tax liens (special priority)
□ Mechanic's liens (filed/recorded)

**Junior Liens:**
□ Survive foreclosure?
□ Merged with senior lien?
□ Purchase money exception?
□ Recording requirements met?

**Special Priorities:**
□ Construction loans (if recorded + used properly)
□ Home equity loans (if properly recorded)
□ Judgment liens (attach to equity)
□ HOA liens (super-priority in some states)

## ADVERSE POSSESSION CHECKLIST

**HATE Elements:**
□ Hostile: without permission
□ Actual: physical use/possession
□ Exclusive: no sharing with owner
□ Continuous: unbroken for period

**Time Requirements:**
□ Color of title: 7 years
□ No color of title: 20 years
□ Tacking: combine periods (privity)
□ Disabilities: toll statute

**Additional Requirements:**
□ Open and notorious
□ Claim of right (majority)
□ Payment of taxes (some states)
□ Good faith belief (some states)

---

## EASEMENT CREATION CHECKLIST

**Express Easement:**
□ Written agreement
□ Signed by parties
□ Clear description of rights
□ Appurtenant vs. in gross

**Implied Easement:**
□ Prior existing use
□ Strict necessity (landlocked)
□ Continuous and obvious
□ At time of severance

**Prescriptive Easement:**
□ Hostile use
□ Actual possession
□ Exclusive to claimant
□ Continuous statutory period
□ Open and notorious

## TAKINGS ANALYSIS CHECKLIST

**Physical Taking:**
□ Government physical invasion?
□ Permanent or temporary?
□ Just compensation required

**Regulatory Taking:**
□ Substantial diminution in value?
□ Penn Central factors
□ Nollan/Dolan nexus
□ Rough proportionality

**Exactions:**
□ Development permit condition?
□ Essential nexus to impact?
□ Roughly proportional?
□ Public purpose

**Defenses:**
□ Police power reasonable
□ No fundamental rights violation
□ Public use requirement
□ Just compensation paid
