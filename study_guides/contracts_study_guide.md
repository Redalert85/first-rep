## 1. FORMATION ‚Äì FOCI CHECKLIST
- **Offer**: Manifestation of willingness to enter bargain.
- **Consideration**: Bargained-for exchange (benefit/detriment). Adequate unless nominal; preexisting duty rule applies (CL).
- **Intent**: Objective manifestations control; ads usually invitations.
- **Acceptance**: CL mirror-image; UCC permissive with additional terms depending on merchant status; mailbox rule.

**Traps & Rules**
- Option contracts (need consideration unless UCC firm offer ‚â§ 3 months, signed by merchant).
- Mailbox rule exceptions: option, improperly addressed, rejection before acceptance.
- Illusory promises not enforceable.

**Mnemonic**: `FOCI` ‚Äì Offer, Consideration, Intent, Acceptance.

**Micro-Hypos**
1. Revocation received before acceptance ‚Üí no contract.
2. Option contract: acceptance effective upon receipt, not dispatch.
3. Illusory promise (‚Äúas many as I want‚Äù) ‚Üí no consideration.

## 2. TERMS & INTERPRETATION
- **Common Law**: Mirror-image; counteroffer rejects.
- **UCC Battle of Forms**: Acceptance with additional/different terms; between merchants, additional terms included unless (i) materially alter, (ii) offer expressly limits acceptance, (iii) objection.
- **Gap Fillers**: Price (reasonable), time (reasonable), place (seller‚Äôs location), payment (due at delivery).
- **Parol Evidence Rule**: Final writing controls; exceptions for ambiguity, fraud, subsequent modification, collateral agreements.
- **Course Hierarchy (UCC)**: Express > Course of performance > Course of dealing > Usage of trade.

**Traps**
- Parol evidence cannot contradict fully integrated writing.
- ‚ÄúNo oral modification‚Äù clauses enforceable under UCC.

---

## 3. PERFORMANCE, BREACH, CONDITIONS
- **Common Law**: Substantial performance; material breach allows withholding.
- **UCC**: Perfect tender rule; single delivery ‚Üí reject for any nonconformity; installment contracts ‚Üí substantial impairment.
- **Conditions**: Precedent vs subsequent; express must be strictly satisfied.
- **Anticipatory Repudiation**: Unequivocal refusal before performance due; non-breaching party may sue, suspend, or demand adequate assurance. Retraction allowed before reliance if no cancellation.
- **Adequate Assurances**: UCC allows written demand if reasonable insecurity; 30-day response window.

**Micro-Hypos**
1. Substantial performance with minor defect ‚Üí damages for deficiency, but no rescission.
2. Installment delivery with minor defect cured ‚Üí rejection improper.
3. Party demands assurance, none given in 30 days ‚Üí anticipatory repudiation.

---

## 4. REMEDIES & DAMAGES (`ERRS`)
- **Expectation**: Place P where performance would.
- **Reliance**: Reimburse expenses.
- **Restitution**: Value of benefit conferred.
- **Specific Performance**: Unique goods/real property; not for personal services (only injunction).

**UCC Damages**
- Buyer Cover: (Cover ‚àí K) + incidental + consequential ‚àí expenses saved.
- Buyer Market: (Market price ‚àí K) + incidental + consequential.
- Buyer Accepted Goods: (Value as warranted ‚àí as accepted) + incidental + consequential.
- Seller Resale: (K ‚àí Resale) + incidental.
- Seller Market: (K ‚àí Market) + incidental.
- Seller Lost Volume: Profit + incidental (must show capacity, profitability, would have made sale).
- Seller Price Action: For goods accepted, risk of loss transferred, or specially manufactured.

**Limits**: Foreseeability (Hadley), certainty, mitigation (employment, cover).
- Liquidated damages valid if difficult to estimate, reasonable prediction.

**Micro-Hypos**
1. Lost volume seller (car dealership) ‚Üí lost profit.
2. Contractor 80% complete; owner breaches ‚Üí expected profit + costs incurred.
3. Invalid penalty clause ‚Üí unenforceable; use actual damages.

---

## 5. DEFENSES (`STUPID`)
- **Statute of Frauds**: Writing signed by party to be charged for Ks not performable within 1 year, marriage, surety, goods ‚â• $500 (UCC), land, executor promises.
- **Unconscionability**: Procedural + substantive; UCC allows court to limit or void.
- **Duress**: Wrongful threat; economic duress if no reasonable alternative.
- **Illegality/Public Policy**: Void to extent of illegality.
- **Mistake**: Mutual (material + basic assumption + risk not assumed); unilateral if non-mistaken party knew or enforcement unconscionable.
- **Impossibility/Impracticability/Frustration**: Supervening event; basic assumption; no fault; no risk allocation.
- **Capacity/Infancy**: Minor can disaffirm; liable for necessities.

**Micro-Hypos**
1. Oral land sale w/ partial performance ‚Üí enforceable despite SOF (possession + payment).
2. Government prohibition after K ‚Üí impossibility.
3. One-party mistake known to other ‚Üí rescission possible.

---

## 6. THIRD-PARTY RIGHTS
- **Intended Beneficiaries**: Rights vest upon assent, suit, or reliance. Promisor can assert defenses against promisee.
- **Assignments**: Transfers rights; for value = irrevocable; gratuitous revocable unless writing delivered. Multiple assignments (majority/Restatement: last bona fide purchaser wins; minority: first assignment wins).
- **Delegations**: Transfer duties. Delegator remains liable unless novation; delegatee liable if assumption of duties.
- **Accord & Satisfaction**: Substitute performance agreement (accord) + completion (satisfaction). Failure ‚Üí can sue on original or accord.
- **Novation**: All parties agree to substitute; original obligor released.

**Micro-Hypos**
1. Intended beneficiary relies ‚Üí rights vest.
2. Gratuitous assignment revoked by subsequent assignment ‚Üí second revokes.
3. Delegatee assumes duties ‚Üí obligee may sue delegatee.

---

## 7. UCC DELIVERY, TITLE & RISK OF LOSS
- Agreement controls first (FOB shipment vs destination terms).
- Breaching party bears risk until cure/acceptance.
- Merchant sellers pass risk on buyer‚Äôs **receipt**; non-merchant sellers upon tender.
- Casualty to identified goods before risk passes voids K.
- Entrustment allows merchant to transfer good title to buyer in ordinary course.
- Voidable title in hands of defrauder ‚Üí can pass good title to good-faith purchaser.

**Micro-Hypos**
1. FOB Dallas; goods destroyed after carrier delivery ‚Üí buyer bears risk.
2. Merchant seller ships nonconforming goods; buyer rejects ‚Üí seller bears risk.
3. Goods destroyed before risk passes, K not yet performed ‚Üí contract avoided.

---

## 8. ACCEPTANCE, REJECTION & REVOCATION (UCC)
- Acceptance via: acknowledging conformity, failing to reject in reasonable time, behaving inconsistently with seller‚Äôs ownership.
- Rejection must be timely; buyer must hold goods with reasonable care.
- Revocation of acceptance allowed when nonconformity substantially impairs value and difficulty of discovery or reliance on cure.
- Right to inspect before acceptance unless COD.
- Adequate assurances available when reasonable grounds for insecurity; failure to respond within 30 days = repudiation.

---

## 9. EXCUSE: IMPOSSIBILITY / IMPRACTICABILITY / FRUSTRATION
- Trigger: supervening event, basic assumption, no fault, risk not assumed.
- UCC: commercial impracticability; extreme shortages due to unforeseen events.
- Partial impracticability ‚Üí allocate fairly among customers, give notice.
- Frustration: principal purpose substantially frustrated.
- Increased cost alone insufficient unless extreme/unreasonable.

---

## 10. MODIFICATION, NOVATION, ACCORD
- **Modification**: CL needs new consideration unless unforeseen; UCC good faith, no consideration required.
- **Accord & Satisfaction**: Performance of accord discharges original duty; failure allows suit on original or accord.
- **Novation**: All parties agree to substitute; original party discharged.

---

## 11. WARRANTIES (UCC & COMMON)
- **Express**: Any affirmation, description, sample basis of bargain.
- **Implied Merchantability**: Merchant sellers; fit for ordinary purpose.
- **Implied Fitness**: Seller knows specific purpose; buyer relies.
- **Disclaimer**: Express hard to disclaim; merchantability must mention term and be conspicuous; fitness must be conspicuous writing; ‚Äúas is‚Äù works.
- **Limitations**: Cannot fail of essential purpose; personal injury limitations unconscionable in consumer goods.
- **Privity/Tort overlap**: Modern trend extends to foreseeable users; strict products liability overlaps.
