# Bar Exam Headline Rules
*Condensed black letter law headlines with precise word counts for quick bar prep review across all MBE subjects.*

---

# PROPERTY LAW

## 1. ESTATES & FUTURE INTERESTS
**Rule (24 words):** Present possessory estates include fee simple absolute, defeasible fees, and life estates; future interests include reversions, remainders, and executory interests that must satisfy RAP.

## 2. CONVEYANCING & RECORDING
**Rule (22 words):** Recording statutes protect bona fide purchasers; race-notice is majority rule, requiring timely recordation without actual or inquiry notice of prior competing interests.

## 3. EASEMENTS, SERVITUDES & COMMON SCHEMES
**Rule (25 words):** Easements grant use rights; equitable servitudes impose negative promises; common schemes bind all subdivision lots where intent, restrictive language, and actual, record, or inquiry notice align.

## 4. CONCURRENT OWNERSHIP
**Rule (20 words):** Joint tenancy requires four unities; tenancy in common has no survivorship; tenancy by entirety protects marital property from separate creditors.

## 5. LANDLORD-TENANT LAW
**Rule (20 words):** Landlord must maintain habitable premises; tenant must pay rent; holdover becomes periodic tenancy; self-help eviction is illegal in most states.

## 6. MORTGAGES, FORECLOSURE & PRIORITY
**Rule (21 words):** Mortgage creates security interest; foreclosure terminates mortgagor's equity; purchase money mortgages enjoy super-priority over later competing liens on the same collateral.

## 7. ZONING & TAKINGS
**Rule (21 words):** Zoning regulates land use; takings require just compensation; variance is exception to zoning rules for hardship; exactions must be roughly proportional.

## 8. ADVERSE POSSESSION
**Rule (21 words):** Hostile, actual, exclusive, continuous possession for statutory period perfects marketable title; color of title reduces the required period to seven years.

## 9. FIXTURES
**Rule (22 words):** Personal property becomes real property when permanently attached with intent to improve the realty; trade fixtures remain personal property for departing tenants.

## 10. WATER LAW & RIPARIAN RIGHTS
**Rule (21 words):** Riparian owners have the right to reasonable domestic and agricultural use of water; prior appropriation doctrine governs priority in western states.

## 11. MINERAL RIGHTS & OIL/GAS LAW
**Rule (21 words):** Mineral rights can be severed from the surface estate; royalty interests and working interests carry distinct development obligations and payment rights.

## 12. SPECIAL PROPERTY INTERESTS
**Rule (22 words):** Future advances, option contracts, and rights of first refusal create enforceable property interests when supported by consideration, recording, and clear triggering conditions.

## 13. EMINENT DOMAIN & INVERSE CONDEMNATION
**Rule (22 words):** Government can take private property for public use with just compensation; inverse condemnation occurs without formal judicial proceedings yet still demands payment.

# CONTRACTS

## 1. CONTRACT FORMATION
**Rule (23 words):** Valid contract requires mutual assent (offer and acceptance), consideration (bargained-for exchange), and no defenses to formation like fraud, duress, or statute of frauds violation.

## 2. OFFER AND ACCEPTANCE
**Rule (24 words):** Offer creates power of acceptance; revocable until accepted unless option contract exists; acceptance must mirror offer under common law (mirror image rule).

## 3. CONSIDERATION
**Rule (22 words):** Consideration requires bargained-for exchange of legal value; past consideration insufficient; modification requires new consideration at common law, not under UCC.

## 4. STATUTE OF FRAUDS
**Rule (25 words):** Contracts for marriage, year+ duration, land sale, executor promises, goods $500+, and surety agreements must be in writing with essential terms and signature.

## 5. PAROL EVIDENCE RULE
**Rule (24 words):** When parties reduce integrated agreement to writing, prior or contemporaneous negotiations cannot vary integrated terms; exceptions include fraud, mistake, condition precedent, and ambiguity.

## 6. MATERIAL BREACH
**Rule (21 words):** Material breach substantially defeats contract's purpose, excusing other party's performance; minor breach allows damages but requires other party to perform completely.

## 7. ANTICIPATORY REPUDIATION
**Rule (23 words):** Clear, unequivocal statement refusing to perform before performance due allows non-breaching party to immediately sue or suspend own performance and await performance date.

## 8. IMPOSSIBILITY & IMPRACTICABILITY
**Rule (25 words):** Performance excused when supervening event destroys subject matter, renders performance illegal, or frustrates principal purpose; impracticability requires extreme unforeseen difficulty exceeding mere economic hardship.

## 9. THIRD PARTY BENEFICIARIES
**Rule (22 words):** Intended beneficiary (donee or creditor) can enforce contract; incidental beneficiary cannot; test requires contracting parties intended to directly benefit third party.

## 10. ASSIGNMENT & DELEGATION
**Rule (24 words):** Contract rights assignable unless materially changes obligor's duty, increases burden, or contract prohibits assignment; duties delegable unless require personal performance or prohibited.

## 11. EXPECTATION DAMAGES
**Rule (21 words):** Puts non-breaching party in position they would occupy had contract been performed; includes direct, consequential, incidental damages minus mitigation and avoidable consequences.

## 12. SPECIFIC PERFORMANCE
**Rule (23 words):** Equitable remedy requiring breaching party to perform; available when legal remedy inadequate (land sales, unique goods); requires definite terms, feasibility, mutuality, fairness.

## 13. UCC ARTICLE 2 DISTINCTIONS
**Rule (25 words):** Governs sale of goods; merchant rules differ from non-merchant; modifications need no consideration; gap-fillers supply missing terms; perfect tender rule replaced by substantial performance.

# TORTS

## 1. NEGLIGENCE ELEMENTS
**Rule (21 words):** Plaintiff must prove defendant owed duty, breached duty, causation (actual and proximate), and damages; all four elements required for prima facie case.

## 2. DUTY OF CARE
**Rule (23 words):** Duty owed to all foreseeable plaintiffs; standard is reasonably prudent person under circumstances; professionals held to professional standard; children to similar age/intelligence.

## 3. BREACH OF DUTY
**Rule (22 words):** Breach determined by balancing risk probability and gravity against burden of precautions; res ipsa loquitur infers negligence when exclusive control and unexplained accident.

## 4. ACTUAL CAUSATION
**Rule (20 words):** But-for test: would injury have occurred but for defendant's conduct? Substantial factor test applies when multiple sufficient causes exist together.

## 5. PROXIMATE CAUSATION
**Rule (23 words):** Limits liability to foreseeable consequences; direct causation presumed proximate; superseding intervening causes break chain when unforeseeable acts occur after defendant's negligence creating new risk.

## 6. COMPARATIVE & CONTRIBUTORY NEGLIGENCE
**Rule (24 words):** Pure comparative reduces recovery by plaintiff's fault percentage; modified bars recovery at 50% or 51%; contributory negligence (minority) bars recovery entirely if any fault.

## 7. BATTERY
**Rule (19 words):** Intentional harmful or offensive contact with plaintiff's person; intent to contact required, not intent to harm; transferred intent applies.

## 8. ASSAULT
**Rule (21 words):** Intentional act causing reasonable apprehension of imminent harmful or offensive contact; requires apparent present ability; words alone generally insufficient absent threatening conduct.

## 9. FALSE IMPRISONMENT
**Rule (21 words):** Intentional confinement within bounded area with no reasonable means of escape; plaintiff must be aware of confinement or suffer harm.

## 10. INTENTIONAL INFLICTION OF EMOTIONAL DISTRESS (IIED)
**Rule (22 words):** Extreme and outrageous conduct intentionally or recklessly causing severe emotional distress; mere insults insufficient; public figures require higher showing of outrageousness.

## 11. DEFAMATION
**Rule (25 words):** Defamatory statement communicated to third party causing reputational harm; public figures require actual malice (knowledge of falsity or reckless disregard); truth is absolute defense.

## 12. STRICT LIABILITY FOR ABNORMALLY DANGEROUS ACTIVITIES
**Rule (23 words):** Liability without fault for non-natural, unusually dangerous activities with significant risk despite reasonable care; cannot be eliminated through due care; common activities excluded.

## 13. PRODUCTS LIABILITY
**Rule (24 words):** Manufacturer/seller strictly liable for defective products causing harm; defect types: manufacturing (departure from design), design (safer alternative design), warning (inadequate instructions/warnings).

# CRIMINAL LAW

## 1. ACTUS REUS & MENS REA
**Rule (21 words):** Crime requires voluntary act (actus reus) plus culpable mental state (mens rea); status crimes unconstitutional; omission liability requires legal duty.

## 2. MENTAL STATES (MPC)
**Rule (25 words):** Purpose: conscious object to engage in conduct or cause result; knowledge: aware of conduct/circumstances; recklessness: conscious disregard of substantial risk; negligence: should be aware.

## 3. COMMON LAW MURDER
**Rule (25 words):** Unlawful killing of human being with malice aforethought: intent to kill, intent to inflict serious bodily injury, depraved heart recklessness, or felony murder.

## 4. FELONY MURDER
**Rule (24 words):** Unintended killing during commission of inherently dangerous felony (BARRK: burglary, arson, robbery, rape, kidnapping); death must be foreseeable consequence; co-felon deaths vary by jurisdiction.

## 5. VOLUNTARY MANSLAUGHTER
**Rule (23 words):** Intentional killing with adequate provocation causing heat of passion; requires no reasonable cooling time and defendant did not actually cool off; "sudden quarrel."

## 6. INVOLUNTARY MANSLAUGHTER
**Rule (19 words):** Unintentional killing through criminal negligence or misdemeanor-manslaughter; requires gross deviation from reasonable care standard; recklessness or gross negligence standard.

## 7. ATTEMPT
**Rule (23 words):** Specific intent to commit target crime plus substantial step beyond mere preparation; impossibility generally no defense except pure legal impossibility; abandonment rarely defense.

## 8. CONSPIRACY
**Rule (25 words):** Agreement between two or more persons to commit unlawful act with intent to agree and achieve objective; majority requires overt act; Pinkerton liability for co-conspirator crimes.

## 9. ACCOMPLICE LIABILITY
**Rule (22 words):** Aiding, abetting, encouraging, or assisting principal with intent to facilitate crime; liable for all foreseeable crimes; mere presence or knowledge insufficient.

## 10. SOLICITATION
**Rule (19 words):** Asking, encouraging, or commanding another to commit crime with intent that crime be committed; refusal or impossibility no defense.

## 11. SELF-DEFENSE
**Rule (25 words):** Reasonable belief of imminent unlawful force justifies proportional force; deadly force requires imminent threat of death/serious bodily harm; duty to retreat in minority jurisdictions.

## 12. DEFENSE OF OTHERS
**Rule (20 words):** May defend others under same circumstances justifying self-defense; reasonable belief test or alter ego rule depending on jurisdiction; proportionality required.

## 13. INSANITY DEFENSES
**Rule (25 words):** M'Naghten: didn't know nature/quality of act or that it was wrong; MPC: lacked substantial capacity to appreciate wrongfulness or conform conduct; irresistible impulse test.

# EVIDENCE

## 1. RELEVANCE (FRE 401)
**Rule (20 words):** Evidence relevant if it has any tendency to make fact of consequence more or less probable; low threshold for admissibility.

## 2. PREJUDICE BALANCING (FRE 403)
**Rule (21 words):** Relevant evidence excluded if probative value substantially outweighed by danger of unfair prejudice, confusion, misleading jury, delay, or waste of time.

## 3. CHARACTER EVIDENCE (FRE 404)
**Rule (25 words):** Character evidence inadmissible to prove propensity (acted in conformity); exceptions: defendant's pertinent trait (opens door), victim's character in self-defense, witness impeachment; MIMIC purposes allowed.

## 4. HABIT EVIDENCE (FRE 406)
**Rule (19 words):** Habit or routine practice admissible to prove conduct on particular occasion; requires specificity and regularity; more admissible than character.

## 5. SUBSEQUENT REMEDIAL MEASURES (FRE 407)
**Rule (21 words):** Post-accident repairs inadmissible to prove negligence or culpable conduct; admissible for ownership, control, feasibility, or impeaching safety claims if disputed.

## 6. HEARSAY RULE (FRE 801)
**Rule (23 words):** Out-of-court statement offered to prove truth of matter asserted is inadmissible unless exception applies; non-hearsay includes prior statements, admissions, and non-truth purposes.

## 7. PRESENT SENSE IMPRESSION (FRE 803(1))
**Rule (20 words):** Statement describing event made while perceiving or immediately thereafter; contemporaneity requirement ensures reliability; no excitement or startling event required unlike excited utterance.

## 8. EXCITED UTTERANCE (FRE 803(2))
**Rule (21 words):** Statement relating to startling event made while under stress of excitement; stress requirement ensures reliability; timing less important than continued excitement state.

## 9. THEN-EXISTING MENTAL/EMOTIONAL CONDITION (FRE 803(3))
**Rule (22 words):** Statement of declarant's then-existing state of mind, emotion, or physical condition admissible; includes intent to do future act; not memory or belief.

## 10. STATEMENT AGAINST INTEREST (FRE 804(b)(3))
**Rule (25 words):** Declarant unavailable; statement so contrary to proprietary, pecuniary, penal, or civil interest reasonable person wouldn't make unless believing true; corroboration required for exculpatory statements.

## 11. DYING DECLARATION (FRE 804(b)(2))
**Rule (21 words):** Declarant unavailable and believed death imminent when making statement about cause or circumstances of impending death; admissible in homicide prosecutions and civil cases.

## 12. FORFEITURE BY WRONGDOING (FRE 804(b)(6))
**Rule (20 words):** Party who wrongfully procured declarant's unavailability forfeits hearsay objection; applies when party intentionally prevented witness from testifying; ensures fairness.

## 13. CONFRONTATION CLAUSE (CRAWFORD)
**Rule (25 words):** Sixth Amendment bars testimonial hearsay unless declarant unavailable and defendant had prior opportunity to cross-examine; non-testimonial hearsay governed by evidence rules alone; formality test.

# CONSTITUTIONAL LAW

## 1. JUDICIAL REVIEW & JUSTICIABILITY
**Rule (24 words):** Federal courts require standing (injury, causation, redressability), ripeness (actual harm), no mootness (live controversy), no political question; state grounds can avoid federal review.

## 2. COMMERCE CLAUSE REGULATION
**Rule (25 words):** Congress can regulate channels of interstate commerce, instrumentalities, persons/things in interstate commerce, and activities substantially affecting interstate commerce when aggregated; cannot regulate inactivity.

## 3. TAXING & SPENDING POWER
**Rule (22 words):** Congress has broad taxing power for revenue or regulatory purposes; spending power permits conditional grants if conditions relate to federal interest and aren't coercive.

## 4. NECESSARY & PROPER CLAUSE
**Rule (20 words):** Congress can enact laws convenient and useful to execute enumerated powers; not independent power source; must serve legitimate enumerated end.

## 5. DORMANT COMMERCE CLAUSE
**Rule (25 words):** States cannot discriminate against interstate commerce or unduly burden it; discrimination requires compelling interest and narrow tailoring; balancing test for evenhanded regulations considers benefits versus burdens.

## 6. TENTH AMENDMENT & ANTI-COMMANDEERING
**Rule (21 words):** Federal government cannot compel states to enact laws, enforce federal programs, or commandeer state legislative/executive processes; encouragement through spending acceptable.

## 7. SUPREMACY CLAUSE PREEMPTION
**Rule (23 words):** Federal law preempts state law when: express preemption, field preemption (comprehensive federal scheme), conflict preemption (impossible to comply with both), or obstacle to objectives.

## 8. PRESIDENTIAL POWERS (YOUNGSTOWN FRAMEWORK)
**Rule (25 words):** Category one: express/implied congressional authorization (strongest); category two: congressional silence (uncertain); category three: contrary to congressional will (weakest); emergency powers limited absent authorization.

## 9. STRICT SCRUTINY
**Rule (21 words):** Applied to fundamental rights and suspect classifications (race, national origin, alienage); requires compelling government interest and narrowly tailored means; rarely satisfied.

## 10. INTERMEDIATE SCRUTINY
**Rule (20 words):** Applied to quasi-suspect classifications (gender, legitimacy); requires important government interest and substantially related means; moderate deference; requires "exceedingly persuasive justification."

## 11. RATIONAL BASIS REVIEW
**Rule (22 words):** Applied to all other classifications and economic regulations; requires legitimate government interest and rationally related means; highly deferential; burden on challenger to negate plausibility.

## 12. PROCEDURAL DUE PROCESS
**Rule (24 words):** Government deprivation of life, liberty, or property requires notice and opportunity to be heard; Mathews balancing test weighs private interest, risk of error, and government burden.

## 13. SUBSTANTIVE DUE PROCESS
**Rule (23 words):** Protects fundamental rights from government interference; includes rights implicit in ordered liberty or deeply rooted in history/tradition; non-fundamental rights receive rational basis review.

## 14. FIRST AMENDMENT - CONTENT-BASED RESTRICTIONS
**Rule (22 words):** Content-based speech restrictions presumptively unconstitutional; subject to strict scrutiny; government must prove compelling interest and narrow tailoring; viewpoint discrimination especially disfavored.

## 15. FIRST AMENDMENT - CONTENT-NEUTRAL RESTRICTIONS
**Rule (23 words):** Time, place, manner restrictions valid if content-neutral, narrowly tailored to significant government interest, and leave ample alternative communication channels; intermediate scrutiny; public forum analysis.

## 16. FIRST AMENDMENT - UNPROTECTED SPEECH
**Rule (25 words):** Categories receiving no protection: incitement to imminent lawless action (Brandenburg), obscenity (Miller test), defamation, fighting words, true threats, child pornography; government may regulate freely.

## 17. FIRST AMENDMENT - PUBLIC FORUM
**Rule (25 words):** Traditional/designated public forums: strict scrutiny for content-based, intermediate for time/place/manner; limited public forums: viewpoint-neutral; non-public forums: reasonable restrictions; private property excludes forum analysis.

## 18. ESTABLISHMENT CLAUSE (LEMON TEST)
**Rule (22 words):** Government action must have secular purpose, primary effect neither advancing nor inhibiting religion, and avoid excessive entanglement; coercion test and historical test alternatives.

## 19. FREE EXERCISE CLAUSE
**Rule (23 words):** Laws targeting religion subject to strict scrutiny; neutral laws of general applicability need only rational basis (Smith); religious exemptions discretionary unless targeting religion.

## 20. FOURTH AMENDMENT - SEARCHES
**Rule (24 words):** Search occurs when government violates reasonable expectation of privacy (Katz test); warrant required unless exception applies: consent, exigent circumstances, automobile, plain view, SILA.

## 21. FIFTH AMENDMENT - TAKINGS
**Rule (24 words):** Government must pay just compensation for taking private property for public use; physical invasions per se taking; regulatory taking requires balancing economic impact, investment expectations, character.

## 22. SIXTH AMENDMENT - RIGHT TO COUNSEL
**Rule (22 words):** Attaches at critical stages after formal charges; includes arraignment, plea, trial, sentencing, appeal as of right; indigent defendants receive appointed counsel.

# CIVIL PROCEDURE

## 1. PERSONAL JURISDICTION - MINIMUM CONTACTS
**Rule (25 words):** Due process requires defendant have minimum contacts with forum so jurisdiction doesn't offend traditional notions of fair play and substantial justice; purposeful availment required; general or specific jurisdiction.

## 2. SUBJECT MATTER JURISDICTION - DIVERSITY
**Rule (24 words):** Complete diversity between plaintiffs and defendants required; citizenship determined by domicile for individuals, incorporation and principal place of business for corporations; exceeds $75,000 exclusive.

## 3. SUBJECT MATTER JURISDICTION - FEDERAL QUESTION
**Rule (21 words):** Federal question appears on face of well-pleaded complaint; federal law creates cause of action; substantial federal issue in state-law claim rarely suffices.

## 4. SUPPLEMENTAL JURISDICTION (28 U.S.C. ยง 1367)
**Rule (25 words):** Federal courts may exercise jurisdiction over state-law claims forming same case or controversy as claims with original jurisdiction; exceptions exist for diversity cases where supplemental claims destroy complete diversity.

## 5. VENUE (28 U.S.C. ยง 1391)
**Rule (23 words):** Proper where any defendant resides (if all reside in same state), substantial part of events occurred, or any defendant subject to personal jurisdiction.

## 6. FRCP 8 PLEADING REQUIREMENTS
**Rule (22 words):** Complaint requires short plain statement of grounds for jurisdiction, claim showing entitlement to relief, and demand for judgment; notice pleading suffices absent fraud/mistake.

## 7. FRCP 12(b)(6) - FAILURE TO STATE CLAIM
**Rule (24 words):** Motion to dismiss granted only if complaint fails to state plausible claim for relief; accept all factual allegations as true; draw inferences favorably to plaintiff (Twombly/Iqbal standard).

## 8. FRCP 11 - SANCTIONS
**Rule (23 words):** Attorneys certify that factual contentions have evidentiary support, legal claims warranted by law or non-frivolous argument, and document not filed for improper purpose.

## 9. FRCP 23 - CLASS ACTIONS
**Rule (25 words):** Requirements: numerosity, commonality, typicality, adequacy of representation; plus Rule 23(b): risk of inconsistent adjudications, injunctive/declaratory relief, or common questions predominate and class action superior.

## 10. FRCP 26 - DISCOVERY SCOPE
**Rule (23 words):** Parties may discover any non-privileged matter relevant to any claim or defense and proportional to case needs; includes hearsay and inadmissible evidence if reasonably calculated.

## 11. FRCP 56 - SUMMARY JUDGMENT
**Rule (24 words):** Granted if no genuine dispute of material fact and movant entitled to judgment as matter of law; non-moving party must show specific evidence; cannot rest on allegations alone.

## 12. RES JUDICATA (CLAIM PRECLUSION)
**Rule (23 words):** Final judgment on merits by court of competent jurisdiction precludes re-litigation of same claim and all claims that could have been brought; transactional test determines claim.

## 13. COLLATERAL ESTOPPEL (ISSUE PRECLUSION)
**Rule (24 words):** Issue actually litigated, necessarily decided, essential to judgment, and same party cannot re-litigate; defensive use allowed if party had full and fair opportunity; offensive use discretionary.
